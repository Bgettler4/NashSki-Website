Pricing & Booking
At NashSki, we offer competitive, transparent pricing for a premium jet ski experience on Old Hickory Lake—just minutes from Nashville.
Our rates vary based on ride duration, availability, and peak times. For real-time pricing and scheduling, use our live booking system to view available time slots and secure your ride instantly.
We’re proud to operate from the east side of Old Hickory Lake, offering one of the most convenient jet ski rental locations in the Greater Nashville area—without the congestion and long idle zones.

Fuel Policy: In line with standard marina practices, fuel is the responsibility of the rider. At check-in, guests will select one of the following options:
* Self-Refuel: Return the jet ski with the same fuel level at the end of your ride
* Convenience Refuel: Allow our team to handle refueling for you at a set fuel rate based on your booked ride time
Fuel usage is estimated and aligned with your rental duration to keep the process simple, transparent, and efficient.

Walk-up rentals are welcome based on availability. Guests who visit us on-site and confirm availability may book directly at the marina, with no additional online booking fees.

Important Things To Know

* You must be at least 18 years old to rent a jet ski.
* The primary driver must be 18+ with a valid government-issued photo ID and a working cell phone for check-in.
* If the driver was born on or after January 1, 1989, a Tennessee Boater Education Card is required to operate.
* If you do not already have your certification, full step-by-step instructions are available on our Boater Certification Guide page (linked below).
* All drivers must present a valid government-issued photo ID at check-in.
* All cancellations must be made at least 24 hours prior to your scheduled reservation time to receive a refund. Cancellations within 24 hours are non-refundable.
* To speed up your check-in process, please complete all required digital waivers prior to arrival (provided at booking).

Boater Certification Checklist (Tennessee Requirement)

(Linked full guide page)

If you were born after January 1, 1989, Tennessee law requires you to complete a boating safety certification before operating a jet ski.
Follow the steps below to get certified quickly and be ready for your NashSki reservation:

1. Confirm You Need Certification
You must complete certification if:
* You were born on or after January 1, 1989
* You plan to operate (drive) a jet ski in Tennessee

2. Complete an Approved Tennessee Boating Safety Course
Take an official Tennessee-approved boating safety course online:
👉 https://www.tn.gov/twra/boating/boating-education.html
* Fully online and self-paced
* Can be completed in 2–4 hours
* Accessible from phone, tablet, or computer
* Must be completed through a TWRA-approved provider

3. Pass the Final Exam
* Complete the online certification exam
* Must pass to receive your Boater Education Card
* Retakes are typically available if needed (provider dependent)

4. Receive Your Boater Education Card
* Issued digitally or by mail depending on provider
* Certification is valid for life
* Must be presented at check-in (digital or physical accepted)

5. Bring Proof to Your Reservation
At check-in with NashSki, you must present:
* Your Boater Education Card
* Valid government-issued photo ID
